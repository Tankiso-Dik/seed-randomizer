## Cultural Vibe

Pigs in Gen Z internet culture (2026) are a **self-deprecating indulgence icon**. The pig emoji (🐷/🐖/🐽) is NOT used literally — it's ironic shorthand for "I'm being a greedy/lazy/messy little piggy and I own it."

**Core Emotional Association:** Playful guilt, cozy indulgence, lazy acceptance, messy-but-proud.

- **🐷 "Pig Energy"** = admitting you ate too much, stayed in bed all day, or did something messy but don't regret it. TikTok 2026: "Just finished my third meal of the day 🐽🐷" — seen on food vlogs.

- **🐽✨ = "Messy pig but make it aesthetic"** — ironic glamour about being unkempt.

### 🪝 B. THE "ME TOO" IDENTITY HOOK

1. **The Human Feeling:** The blissful surrender of total indulgence with zero guilt. That specific moment after you've eaten way too much, or done absolutely nothing productive all day, and instead of feeling shame, you just feel deeply, unreasonably *content*. The pleasure of saying "I regret nothing" and meaning it.

2. **The "Why Wear It":** The wearer signals unapologetic, guilt-free self-acceptance. "I own my indulgences and I'm not sorry about it." This is a badge for anyone who rejects the shame cycle around food, rest, or mess — a statement of defiant contentment.

3. **The Punchline:** A pig that has clearly over-indulged to the point of total physical collapse (limbs splayed, chin on floor, zero structural integrity) but is wearing a HUGE blissful smile — and the surrounding text frame insists "I REGRET NOTHING." The contrast between the extreme state of collapse and the shameless declaration is the joke.

### 🖼️ F. THE MASTER COMPOSITION PROMPT

**PART 1: The Medium & Format (Setting the Canvas)**

> A flat screenprint-style t-shirt graphic on a transparent background of a pig, designed as a Format B Bold Text Frame — the text phrase forms a thick continuous decorative border around the pig, and the pig is centered inside the frame on a 3:4 portrait-oriented apparel canvas.

**PART 2: The Subject & Emotional Paradox (The "Me Too" Hook - Fully Described)**

> A pig lying in a completely collapsed, boneless heap — flat on its belly with its chin resting directly on the ground. The pig has all four limbs splayed out limply in different directions like a starfish, conveying zero structural integrity and total post-indulgent surrender. The pig's eyes are fully closed in happy crescent-shaped squints, with a huge wide open-mouthed smile showing a few teeth, cheeks pushed up high, and the whole face lifted in pure bliss, conveying a sense of serene delivery of chaotic content — a creature that has clearly over-indulged but feels absolutely zero shame about it.

**PART 3: The Physical Weight, Static Geometry & Natural Asymmetry (Defeating AI Hallucinations)**

> The pig is frozen in a static, collapsed pose with no active movement or motion blur. The weight is distributed completely flat with a natural asymmetry — one front leg is splayed slightly more outward than the other, the head is tilted 8 degrees to one side. Exactly two front legs and two back legs are visible, all clearly separated from the round body. Simple, chunky cartoon hooves with NO individual toes or fingers. The pig's snout is a simple oval shape. Two floppy ears, simple rounded shapes, one flopped forward more than the other for natural asymmetry. No extra limbs, no extra body parts. The tail is a small tight curl, simplified, not a long thin spiral.

**PART 4: The Typography, Text Isolation, & Spelling Shield (Defeating AI Text Mistakes)**

> The text phrase "I REGRET NOTHING" is written in a bold collegiate varsity block font with a simple solid black outline, set in ALL CAPS with no punctuation. Letters are a solid warm cream color with no patterns, gradients, or textures inside. The text forms a thick continuous rectangular border frame around the pig, completely separated from the pig by wide empty negative space. The text does not wrap around, overlap, or touch the pig. Plain flat 2D lettering only, no 3D text, no 3D extrusion, no drop shadows on text, no isometric lettering, no warp or slant, no spelling mistakes. The word "NOTHING" does not break across multiple lines awkwardly.

**PART 5: The Rendering Shield & Color Cohesion (The Bold Mascot in Vintage Screen Print Style Lock)**

> Color palette: warm cream (#F5F0E8), vintage peachy pink (#D4858C), charcoal black (#2D2D2D). Flat colors only, bold color blocking, no gradients. The pig's body is solid vintage peachy pink. The pig's inner ears, snout highlight, and belly highlight are warm cream. The text letters are warm cream with charcoal black outlines. The pig is surrounded by clean transparent space. Grounded simplified mascot anatomy (70% animal, 30% stylization). Thick, confident uniform black outlines. Stipple/halftone shading texture on the pig's body combined with visible screen print ink texture, slight misregistration effects, and deliberate alignment imperfections to create an authentic vintage athletic screen print/patch feel. Background: TRANSPARENT.

**PART 6: The Negative Constraints (Cleaning the Edges)**

> No mockup, no shirt shown, isolated graphic only, transparent background. NO PROPS — the pig is not wearing, holding, or interacting with any objects. Avoid photorealism, realistic anatomy, realistic pig fur or bristles, over-detailed illustration, thin outlines, clean digital lines, watercolor, smooth gradients, glossy rendering, 3D rendering, realistic lighting, shadows beyond flat screen-print stipple. STRICTLY AVOID 3D text, 3D extrusion, drop shadows on text, isometric lettering, cursive fonts, italic text, script fonts, overly melting or noodly anatomy, complex fingers or toes, mechanical props, text-heavy props, 3D props, solid background colors, gradient backgrounds, gradients anywhere, multiple characters, scenery, multiple text elements, decorative flourishes, decorative borders beyond the text frame itself.
