## Phase 1: Context Brief

**The Seed (Animal + Emotion)**
- **Animal:** Pig
- **Direction:** Delusional / Absurd

**The Cultural Vibe**
The internet's perception of "pigs" in an absurd or delusional context flows down two distinct, hyper-niche rabbit holes:
1. **The "Öats" Lore (Surreal / Philosophical):** Originating from a 19th-century British folk art painting of two freakishly round, long pigs. The internet spun a deeply surreal, pseudo-philosophical lore where one pig ("Hogcrates") begs the other ("Oinkytus") for oats ("Bröther, may I have some öats?"). The oat-hoarder is delusional, believing he is favored by the "tall, skinny gods" (human farmers), while the other warns of the "Shed of No Return" (the slaughterhouse). It’s a perfect blend of high-art aesthetics and existential dread/greed.
2. **Modern Brainrot (Hyper-Ironic):** Zoomer/Alpha TikTok trends like "John Pork" (an anthropomorphic pig influencer who ominously "calls" you) and Italian brainrot ("Svinino Bombondino"), characterized by chaotic visuals, glitch aesthetics, and nonsensical soundboards.

**The Format Route**
**Format:** T-Shirt
**Reasoning:** The "Oats" Lore perfectly aligns with the current trend of putting classical/vintage art on oversized, heavyweight tees with highly ironic, text-heavy graphics (similar to underground metal or bootleg streetwear). 

**Raw Concept Angles**
1. **"The Shed of No Return" (Heavy Metal / Vintage Bootleg):** A distressed, vintage graphic tee featuring the 19th-century folk-art pigs, styled like an underground metal band tour shirt. Jagged, dark text reads: "SHED OF NO RETURN" and "BEWARE THE TALL SKINNY ONES."
2. **"The Illusion of Oats" (Minimalist / Avant-Garde):** A clean, high-fashion piece (think A24 merch). A singular, extremely fat/long pig centered in the graphic, with tiny, elegant serif text below: "Bröther, may I have some öats?" and "The Platonic Cave of Swine."
3. **"John Pork is Calling" (Y2K / Cyber Brainrot):** A chaotic, early-2000s cyber-aesthetic design. An anthropomorphic pig holding a flip phone, surrounded by early Windows-style error pop-ups and glitch art. Text: "He is calling. Will you answer?"
4. **"Oat Gluttony" (Tapestry / Tarot Style):** The two pigs drawn in the style of a medieval tarot card or woodblock print. The delusional pig sits atop a mountain of oats. The card reads "THE DELUSIONAL" or "THE FOOL", symbolizing blind greed in the face of the inevitable slaughter.
