# MASTER WORKFLOW CONTEXT

## Phase 1: Context Brief

**The Seed**
- **Animal:** Frog

**The Cultural Vibe**
The internet's depiction of frogs splits into three major subcultural lanes:
1. **Unhinged/IBS Humor (Tummy Ache Survivor):** A massive Gen Z/Millennial wave centering around anxiety, somatic symptoms, and anti-system frustration. Exemplified by the viral bumper sticker/shirt "My tummy hurts and I'm mad at the government" and "Not Now Partner, My Tummy Hurts" (cowboy frog). The vibe is low-stakes misery mixed with political disillusionment.
2. **Existential / Burnout Core ("Rage Consumes Me"):** Small, cute green frogs expressing massive, overwhelming emotions (rage, panic, existential dread). Often paired with medieval marginalia aesthetic (goblincore, weirdcore) or cozy cottagecore styles where the frog is reading or having tea but is secretly distressed.
3. **Chronic Overthinking / Introvert battery:** "I've reached my limit for today" frog, representing social exhaustion.

**The Format Route**
- **Format:** T-Shirt / Sticker
- **Reasoning:** Frog-themed designs have massive sticker-collector and casual-tee markets. Leaning into the unhinged anxiety / overthinking vibe captures the ironic streetwear trend.

**Market Demand Signals**
- Bestsellers on Etsy for "Embroidered Frog Shirt - Rage Consumes Me" and "Comfort Colors Self Care Frog Shirt".
- Redbubble listings for "My tummy hurts and I'm mad at the government" have thousands of favorites and high review volumes.
- TikTok compilations of "funny frog memes 2026" and "giant frog block takeover" showcase high viral interest.

**Phrase Templates**
1. `"Panic First, Function Later."` (Confessional / Unapologetic template: `[Verb] First, [Verb] Later`)
2. `"Diagnosed With Chronic Overthinking."` (Bold Label / Clinical template: `Diagnosed With [Condition]`)
3. `"Panic. Cry. Nap."` (Rule of 3 / Escalating template: `[Verb]. [Verb]. [Verb].`)

**Long-Tail Opportunities**
- "Not Now Partner My Tummy Hurts" (target: cowboy frog, lactose intolerance, IBS)
- "Rage Consumes Me Frog" (target: anger issues, cute green frog)
- "Mentally Somewhere Else Frog" (target: space out, daydreaming)

**Competitive Landscape Summary**
- Competitor listings feature cute, simple vector frogs or detailed watercolor frogs.
- Competitors heavily target "frog lover," "cute frog," "cottagecore frog" keywords.
- Gap: There is a lack of high-contrast "Vintage Screen Print / Retro Athletic Mascot" styles representing *existential/panic* humor, which are usually depicted in simple line art or MS Paint styles.

**Competitive Saturation:** Medium-High (General frog shirts are saturated, but the "anxious/cynical vintage mascot" niche is underserved).

**Gap Opportunity:** Bring a premium, stipple-shaded athletic mascot aesthetic to an anxious/overwhelmed frog design, subverting the typical "tough" athletic mascot with pure panic/anxiety.

**Keyword Repetition Blueprint:**
- **Main Tag:** `anxious frog`
- **Repetition strategy:** Repeat `anxious frog` in Title, Main Tag, Description, and Tags.

**Market Intent Confidence Score:** High (frog memes and burnout humor are evergreen for the Gen Z/Millennial POD demographic).

**Raw Concept Angles**
1. **"Panic First, Function Later" (The Distressed Athlete):** An anxious frog with bulging eyes and a sweat drop, dressed in a retro athletic jersey or running gear, looking completely overwhelmed.
2. **"Rage Consumes Me" (Medieval Woodcut):** A tiny, angry frog with a tiny sword and shield, shouting in mid-scream with a funny angry face, in a woodcut/stipple print format.
3. **"Clinically Overthinking" (The Thinker):** A frog sitting on a rock in the classic "Thinker" pose, but with a wide-eyed, sleepless thousand-yard stare and hands holding its temples.

## Phase 2: The Art Director's Concept & Prompt

**1. The "Me Too" Identity Hook**
- **The Human Feeling:** The sudden, overwhelming surge of panic and anxiety when attempting basic daily tasks.
- **The "Why Wear It":** Signaling: "I am trying my best to function, but I am in a constant state of low-key panic." Connects with work-from-home, student, or corporate burnout.
- **The Punchline:** An athletic mascot (which is supposed to represent determination and power) depicted as a chubby green frog suffering from immediate panic and existential dread.

**2. The Phrase**
"PANIC FIRST. FUNCTION LATER." (The Confessional Framework — Unapologetic Register)

**3. The Style Anchors**
- **Composition:** Format C (Grounded Mascot + Arched Banner) — mascot in lower 60%, massive arched text in upper 40%.
- **Color Palette:** Forest & Earth (sage green #7A8B7B, muted red #B85A5A, cream #FDFBF7, and charcoal black #1E1E1E)
- **Typography:** Bold collegiate varsity block letters, all-caps
- **Environment:** Completely isolated/transparent background. One approved hero prop: a simple sweat headband.

**4. Sanity Check Results**
- **Length Limit:** 4 words (Passes maximum 8 words rule).
- **Spice Factor:** Cynical, self-aware, and internet-native (Passes).
- **The "Pinterest" Test:** Not a wholesome farmhouse sign. Cynical and funny (Passes).
- **Show, Don't Tell:** The phrase describes the priorities, the frog's wide-eyed sweat drop face and slumped shoulders deliver the punchline (Passes).
- **Hero Prop Constraints:** Max 1 prop (sweat headband) sitting askew on the head, flat 2D shape, no banned features (Passes).

**5. The Master Composition Prompt**
> "A flat screenprint-style t-shirt graphic on a transparent background of a Frog, designed as a Format C Grounded Mascot with Arched Banner. A chubby green frog wearing a simple red-and-white striped sweat headband on its head. The frog has bulging, wide-eyed circular eyes with tiny black pupils staring straight ahead in a thousand-yard panic stare, mouth slightly agape in a silent scream, with exactly three cartoon sweat droplets flying off its head, conveying a sense of immediate distress and anxiety. The frog stands in a slumped, off-balance posture with shoulders slouched forward, head tilted 10 degrees to the right. Exactly two front arms dangle limply at its sides, clearly separated from the round torso. Exactly two thick, squat back legs are bent slightly at the knees, grounded on the bottom edge. Simple chunky cartoon paws and feet with NO individual fingers or toes. No extra limbs, no active motion. The text phrase "PANIC FIRST. FUNCTION LATER." is written in a massive arched banner at the top, in a flat, bold collegiate varsity block font with a thick solid black outline and solid cream fill. Text is positioned in a semi-circle arching over the frog, completely separated from the subject by empty negative space. A clean negative space boundary separates the text from the graphic. Plain flat 2D lettering only, no 3D extrusion, no drop shadows on text, no spelling mistakes. Color palette: sage green (#7A8B7B), muted red (#B85A5A), cream (#FDFBF7), and charcoal black (#1E1E1E). Flat colors only, bold color blocking, no gradients. The green of the frog matches the sage green details, and the red of the headband matches the red outlines of the arched banner for color harmony. Grounded simplified mascot anatomy (70% animal, 30% stylization). Thick, uniform black outlines. Stipple/halftone shading texture combined with visible screen print ink texture and deliberate alignment/texture imperfections. Background: TRANSPARENT. No mockup, no shirt shown, isolated graphic only. One sweat headband is the only prop, no other objects. Avoid photorealism, realistic anatomy, realistic skin, over-detailed illustration, thin outlines, clean digital lines, watercolor, smooth gradients, glossy rendering. STRICTLY AVOID 3D text, 3D extrusion, drop shadows on text, isometric lettering, cursive fonts, melting or noodle limbs, complex fingers/toes."
