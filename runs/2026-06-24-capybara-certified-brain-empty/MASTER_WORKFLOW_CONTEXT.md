### 🖼️ Master Composition Prompt

**1. [The Medium & Format (Setting the Canvas)]**
> *"A flat screenprint-style t-shirt graphic on a transparent background of a capybara, designed as a Format A Suspicious Close-Up — tight crop to the head and shoulders with the capybara staring directly at the viewer."*

**2. [The Subject & Emotional Paradox (The "Me Too" Hook — Fully Described)]**
> *"A capybara with a wide, unblinking stare — both eyes fully open and round, pupils small and centered, one eye slightly wider than the other for natural asymmetry. Mouth is slightly agape, lips parted in a look of gentle, permanent confusion. Head is tilted at approximately 45 degrees to the right. A single small orange is perfectly balanced on top of the capybara's head, completely ignored by the animal. The capybara's posture is perfectly still and serene, conveying a sense of serene delivery of chaotic content — total calm paired with the mildly unhinged admission of having nothing behind the eyes."*

**3. [The Physical Weight, Static Geometry & Natural Asymmetry (Defeating AI Hallucinations)]**
> *"The mascot is frozen in a static, grounded pose. Head tilted 45 degrees to the right. One eye is slightly wider than the other. The neck merges into broad, rounded shoulders. Exactly two front paws are visible at the bottom of the frame, resting limp and still. Simple, chunky cartoon paws with NO individual fingers or toes. Only one visible limb pair — two front paws in frame. No visible back legs. The orange sits flat on the top of the head, a simple circle with a tiny stem, not interacting with the capybara at all. No extra limbs, no overlapping body parts creating visual confusion."*

**4. [The Typography, Text Isolation, & Spelling Shield (Defeating AI Text Mistakes)]**
> *"The text phrase 'CERTIFIED BRAIN EMPTY' is written in a flat, bold condensed collegiate varsity block font with a simple solid charcoal black fill and no outline. Letters are solid charcoal black with no patterns, no gradients, no textures. Text is positioned below the capybara in two lines — 'CERTIFIED' on the top line, 'BRAIN EMPTY' on the bottom line — centered horizontally. The text is completely separated from the subject by empty negative space. Clean negative space boundary separates the text from the graphic. The text does not wrap around, overlap, or touch the animal. Plain flat 2D lettering only, no 3D text, no 3D extrusion, no drop shadows on text, no spelling mistakes, no text on the orange or anywhere else."*

**5. [The Rendering Shield & Color Cohesion (The Bold Mascot in Vintage Screen Print Style Lock)]**
> *"Color palette: cream (warm off-white for capybara body base), charcoal black (thick uniform outlines, text, pupils, nostril), burnt orange (the orange on the head), warm taupe/tan (fur stipple shading and ear interiors). Flat colors only, bold color blocking, no gradients. The colors of the text match the charcoal black outline color of the animal for visual harmony. Grounded simplified mascot anatomy — 70% animal, 30% stylization, with a larger head, smaller body, and simplified rounded shapes. Thick, confident uniform black outlines. Stipple/halftone dot shading texture in warm taupe combined with visible screen print ink texture and deliberate alignment/texture imperfections to create an authentic vintage athletic screen print/patch feel. Background: TRANSPARENT."*

**6. [The Negative Constraints (Cleaning the Edges)]**
> *"No mockup, no shirt shown, isolated graphic only, transparent background. One small burnt orange sitting flat on top of the head is the only prop — no other objects, no backgrounds, no scenery, no frames. Avoid photorealism, realistic fur textures, realistic animal anatomy, over-detailed illustration, thin outlines, clean digital vector lines, watercolor, smooth gradients, glossy rendering, airbrushing. STRICTLY AVOID 3D text, 3D extrusion, drop shadows on text, isometric lettering, cursive fonts, overly melting or noodly anatomy, complex fingers or toes, mechanical props, text-heavy props, 3D props, solid background colors, multiple props, crowded compositions."*
